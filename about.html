About

I’m a software engineer with a strong focus on building developer tooling, backend frameworks, and systems-level software. I’m particularly interested in how low-level design decisions affect ergonomics, performance, and long-term maintainability, and I enjoy working on projects that sit at the boundary between infrastructure and developer experience.

A large part of my work revolves around modern JavaScript and TypeScript runtimes, especially Bun. I maintain and experiment with several projects in this space, including HTTP frameworks, templating tools, and native bindings. Through this work, I’ve gained hands-on experience designing APIs, implementing routing and middleware systems, and thinking carefully about how developers interact with libraries at scale.

Alongside web and tooling work, I spend time exploring systems programming concepts such as compiler construction, native interop, and low-level libraries. I invest in doing hobby projects while I have down time to increase my ability for when I am in a production environment.

I value clarity, explicitness, and pragmatism in software design. I prefer simple, well-documented systems over clever abstractions, and I aim to build tools that are easy to reason about, easy to extend, and hard to misuse. Whether I’m working on a framework, a programming experiment, or a small utility, my goal is to create software that other developers can confidently build on and consumers to intuitively use.
